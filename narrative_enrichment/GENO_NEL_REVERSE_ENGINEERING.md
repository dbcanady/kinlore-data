## PART 2: THE 50 NARRATIVE INFERENCES — The NEL's Raw Material

The Narrative Inference Catalog (Mar 9, 2026) produced 50 structured inferences plus 7 emergent patterns. These are NOT stories — they are the intermediate layer between structured data and narrative. Each inference has:

- **Fact** (the data point)
- **Inference** (the narrative conclusion)
- **Reasoning Chain** (the logic bridge)
- **Evidence Type** (census, vital, military, land, absence, etc.)
- **Sociological Framework** (migration corridor, economic shock, naming convention, etc.)
- **Confidence** (near-certain / probable / speculative)
- **Narrative Temperature** (cold / warm / hot)
- **Reusability** (person-specific vs. generalizable rule)

The 7 emergent patterns are:

| Pattern | Name | Key Insight |
|---------|------|-------------|
| A | Economic Gravity Model | Families move toward functional economies, not prosperity |
| B | Paper Trail Paradox | The people with the most records are often the ones in the most trouble |
| C | The Census Taker Effect | Spellings are artifacts of the recorder, not the recorded |
| D | Military Records as Biographical ATMs | Military service produces more biographical data than any other life event |
| E | The Genealogical Telephone Game | Each retelling of a family story adds error — Levi at Gettysburg, Harvey Knauss, etc. |
| F | Asymmetry of Documentary Survival | Depth is a function of record-keeping culture, not family importance |
| G | Migration Trigger Is Always Arithmetic | Strip away romance: every migration is "can we eat here?" |

## PART 3: REVERSE-ENGINEERING THE NEL

### What the NEL Actually Does (Observed from 42 Stories)

Looking backwards at what was produced, the NEL performs five distinct operations:

#### Operation 1: RECORD SELECTION
**Input:** Person contract (structured data with evidence tags)
**Output:** The 1–3 records that contain the most narratively interesting information

Not all records are created equal. The NEL doesn't narrate the census that says "age 36, farmer, North Carolina." It narrates the one where the age is wrong, or the occupation changed, or the household composition reveals something unexpected. The selection criteria observed across all 42 stories:

- **Contradiction records** get selected first (John H. Canady's age lie, George Knauss's three conflicting birth years)
- **Absence records** get selected second (Thomas Boon in the poorhouse — poverty documented by charity)
- **Transition records** get selected third (Daniel going from "Farmer" to "Gentleman"; Henry going from "Turpentiner" to soldier)
- **Routine confirming records** are used for context but never drive the narrative

**NEL Rule:** `IF record.contains_contradiction OR record.documents_absence OR record.shows_transition THEN priority = HIGH`

#### Operation 2: FRAMEWORK MATCHING
**Input:** Selected records + person metadata (geography, era, occupation, ethnicity)
**Output:** The sociological framework that makes the record legible to a modern reader

This is where the 7 emergent patterns become operational. The NEL matches each person to the framework that explains their world:

| Person Metadata | Framework Selected | Example |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| Occupation = turpentiner, Geography = Cape Fear | Naval Stores Extraction Economy | Henry Canady (Inference 001) |
| Multiple locations across decades | Economic Gravity Model / Migration Arithmetic | George Knauss (Inference 011–012) |
| Military service record exists | Military Records as Biographical ATM | Abel Bass, Evans Canady, William Boon |
| Name appears in 3+ variant spellings | Census Taker Effect | Daniel Canady/Kennedy/Kundy |
| Record contradicts family oral tradition | Genealogical Telephone Game | Levi at Gettysburg, Harvey Knauss |
| Person from non-English-speaking origin | Documentary Survival Asymmetry | Jurjorsic dead end, Norwegian records available |
| Person appears in poorhouse/pension/charity records | Paper Trail Paradox | Thomas Boon, Abel Bass pension at 85 |

**NEL Rule:** `MATCH person.metadata AGAINST framework_library → RETURN framework[0] (best fit) + framework[1] (secondary)`

#### Operation 3: TEMPERATURE SCORING
**Input:** Selected records + matched framework + person's position in tree
**Output:** Narrative temperature assignment (cold / warm / hot)

The Narrative Inference Catalog defined temperature as:
- **Cold:** Informational, establishes context
- **Warm:** Adds human texture, makes the reader care
- **Hot:** The thing that makes someone call their mother or buy the White Glove tier

From the 42 stories, temperature correlates with:

| Temperature | Trigger Condition | Example |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| HOT | Record contains a human decision with consequences visible in later records | John H. Canady lying about his age → married at 16 → first child within the year |
| HOT | Record documents a moment of irreversible loss | William Boon killed 61 days after enlisting; Henry Clay Boone dies at 44, Ruth is 4 |
| HOT | Record reveals something the person probably didn't want known | Frances Chason's pension testimony reveals the Tennessee detour nobody documented |
| WARM | Record shows economic calculation or survival strategy | Rosa Haney's remarriage; Abel Bass filing pension at 85 |
| WARM | Record connects to a living person's identity | Steve Knauss's oral history about Kansas/Missouri |
| COLD | Record confirms what we already expected | Census showing expected occupation for expected geography |

**NEL Rule:** `IF record.reveals_hidden_decision → HOT; IF record.shows_survival_strategy → WARM; IF record.confirms_expectation → COLD`

#### Operation 4: NARRATIVE PATTERN SELECTION
**Input:** Temperature score + framework + record type
**Output:** The narrative structure (how to tell it)

From the 42 stories, I can identify 13 distinct narrative patterns the NEL uses:

| Pattern ID | Pattern Name | When It Fires | Example Story |
|------------|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| NP-01 | Brick Wall Narrative | Person is a TPA; story is about what we DON'T know | Daniel Canady (#1) |
| NP-02 | Source Contradiction as Plot | Two records disagree on a material fact | Child Groom (#2) |
| NP-03 | Paper Trail as Character Study | Extensive bureaucratic record reveals personality | E.A. Canady Estate (#3) |
| NP-04 | Single Document Deep Read | One record, exhaustively analyzed | Henry's Bounty (#4) |
| NP-05 | Military Journey Arc | Enlistment → service → wound/capture → aftermath | Abel Bass (#6), William Boon (#8) |
| NP-06 | Economic Failure as Story | Court records, bankruptcy, lawsuits | A.W. Bass Sawmill (#7) |
| NP-07 | Negative Evidence Narrative | Poverty, absence, what WASN'T recorded | Thomas Boon (#9) |
| NP-08 | Remarriage as Economic Decision | Widow/widower remarries; pension implications | Rosa Haney (#10) |
| NP-09 | Migration Narrative | Triple Squeeze / Economic Gravity / Chain Migration | George Knauss (#21) |
| NP-10 | Naming Convention as Clue | A name (middle, given, or surname variant) reveals a hidden connection | Charles Bloxum Boone (#19) |
| NP-11 | Convergence Narrative | Multiple lines converge at one place/time | 2201 Progress Street (#20) |
| NP-12 | Audience-Adapted Summary | Same data, rewritten for a different reader | Nephew document (#37) |
| NP-13 | Gift Narrative | Custom output for a living person using ancestor data | Steve's Birthday (#28) |

**NEL Rule:** `SELECT narrative_pattern FROM pattern_library WHERE pattern.triggers MATCH (temperature, framework, record_type)`

#### Operation 5: CONFIDENCE GATING
**Input:** All narrative content generated
**Output:** Content with appropriate hedging, caveats, or explicit uncertainty markers

This is the editorial layer that prevents the NEL from inventing facts. Observed rules:

- **Near-certain facts** get stated declaratively: "Abel Bass enlisted in the 20th Regiment NC."
- **Probable inferences** get "likely/probably" qualifiers: "George probably left because the arithmetic didn't work."
- **Speculative inferences** get explicit framing: "We don't know why they went to Tennessee."
- **Corrected errors** get flagged aggressively: "Levi did NOT serve at Gettysburg."

**NEL Rule:** `IF confidence = 'speculative' THEN wrap_in_uncertainty(); IF confidence = 'corrected_error' THEN flag_prominently()`

## PART 4: THE NEL ARCHITECTURE (Reverse-Engineered)

```
INPUT: Person Contract (structured JSON)
  │
  ├─► [1] RECORD SELECTOR
  │     Scans source_records[]
  │     Prioritizes: contradictions > absences > transitions > confirmations
  │     Output: selected_records[1..3]
  │
  ├─► [2] FRAMEWORK MATCHER  
  │     Matches person metadata against framework_library (7+ frameworks)
  │     Considers: geography, era, occupation, ethnicity, record_types_available
  │     Output: primary_framework, secondary_framework
  │
  ├─► [3] TEMPERATURE SCORER
  │     Evaluates each selected record for narrative heat
  │     Considers: hidden decisions, irreversible loss, survival strategies, living connections
  │     Output: temperature_score per record (cold/warm/hot)
  │
  ├─► [4] PATTERN SELECTOR
  │     Matches (temperature + framework + record_type) to narrative_pattern_library (13 patterns)
  │     Output: narrative_pattern_id
  │
  ├─► [5] NARRATIVE GENERATOR
  │     Produces prose using selected pattern
  │     Applies Kilgore writing style (if configured)
  │     Applies audience adaptation (if target_audience specified)
  │     Output: raw_narrative
  │
  └─► [6] CONFIDENCE GATE
        Reviews all assertions in raw_narrative against evidence_tags
        Inserts hedging language where confidence < near-certain
        Flags corrected errors prominently
        Output: final_narrative
```

### Tier Mapping (Product Implication)

| Tier | What the NEL Produces | Temperature Threshold |
|------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Free / Discovery** | Cold facts only — dates, places, names | All temperatures visible, narrative = summary only |
| **Standard** | Warm narratives — the "your ancestors were real people" layer | Warm + Cold; Hot hooks visible as teasers |
| **White Glove** | Full hot narratives — the "call your mother" stories | All temperatures; full narrative patterns; Gift Narratives enabled |

### Schema Fields the NEL Consumes

From the Person Contract Schema (designed Mar 9):

- `identity.name_variants[]` → feeds Census Taker Effect framework + NP-10
- `source_records[].claims[]` → feeds Record Selector (Operation 1)
- `confidence.composite_badge` → feeds Confidence Gate (Operation 6)
- `enrichment_flags[]` → feeds Framework Matcher (Operation 2)
- `narrative_threads[]` → **this is actually the NEL's output**, stored back into the contract
- `narrative_threads[].tier` → maps to product tier (core / white_glove / research_only)
- `narrative_threads[].evidence_strength` → maps to confidence gating

### What's Missing from the Current Schema for NEL

1. **No `narrative_temperature` field on individual source records.** The NEL needs to score each record's heat independently before selecting which records to narrate. Currently, temperature lives only in the Inference Catalog, not in the person contract.

2. **No `framework_tags[]` array on the person contract.** The Framework Matcher output needs somewhere to live so downstream processes know which sociological context applies. Currently this is implicit in `enrichment_flags` but should be explicit.

3. **No `audience` parameter on narrative_threads.** The same person's data produces different narratives for different audiences (nephew vs. researcher vs. White Glove customer). The schema should support multiple narrative_threads per person, differentiated by audience.

4. **No `reusability_score` on inferences.** The 50-inference catalog distinguished person-specific inferences from generalizable rules. The NEL needs this to know when an inference can be applied to OTHER people matching similar conditions vs. only to this specific person.

5. **No `related_stories[]` cross-reference.** Story #20 (2201 Progress Street) explicitly depends on stories #1–19 converging. The NEL needs a way to express narrative dependencies between person contracts.

## PART 5: WHAT ENGINEERING BACKWARDS ACTUALLY REVEALS

### The Most Important Finding

The NEL is not a single system. It's a **pipeline of five discrete operations** (select → match → score → pattern → generate → gate), each of which can be built, tested, and iterated independently. The GENO project accidentally built the test suite by producing 42 stories and 50 inferences — every one of those is a regression test for the pipeline.

### The Second Most Important Finding

**The narrative patterns are finite and enumerable.** There are 13 of them. Not 130, not "infinite creativity." Thirteen patterns that, in combination with 7 frameworks and 3 temperature levels, produce the full range of observed narrative output. This means the NEL is engineerable — it's not magic, it's a decision tree with a prose generator at the end.

### The Third Most Important Finding

**The schema already has most of what the NEL needs.** The person contract schema designed on Mar 9 contains `narrative_threads[]`, `enrichment_flags[]`, `confidence` scoring, and `source_records[]` with evidence tags. The five gaps identified above (temperature on records, framework tags, audience parameter, reusability score, cross-references) are additions, not redesigns.

### The Kill Shot

If you can automate Operations 1–4 (Record Selection, Framework Matching, Temperature Scoring, Pattern Selection), then Operation 5 (Narrative Generation) is just an LLM prompt with the right context window. The hard engineering is in the decision-making, not the prose. The prose is the easy part — you've already proven that with 42 stories generated from structured data in a chat interface.

The NEL isn't a "writing engine." It's a **classification engine with a writer at the end.**
